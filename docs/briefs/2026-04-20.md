## Macro Cycle Assessment

The US economy looks to be in **late-cycle mid-expansion**. The yield curve has re-steepened to +55 bps from inversion during 2022–24, which historically tracks a post-recession-scare rebound. Unemployment sits at 4.3% (March print) — up from 3.5% two years ago but still low; payroll growth of +178k in March is a cooling-but-healthy pace. Headline CPI jumped to 3.3% YoY in March, but that move is heavily driven by the Iran-war gasoline spike (gasoline +21.2% m/m); core CPI is only 2.6% YoY, indicating underlying disinflation is intact.

Risk assets confirm the late-cycle read: the S&P 500 made a record close on April 17 at 7,126 (+35% YoY), credit spreads have compressed to cycle lows, and the VIX at 17.5 signals complacency returning. With the Fed holding at 3.50–3.75% and still-positive real yields, policy is modestly restrictive. The risk is that an inflation restart (oil-driven) delays further easing — but the sharp WTI reversal after the Iran ceasefire lowers that risk meaningfully.

## Allocation Recommendations

**US Large Cap Equities — Neutral.** Earnings are strong and the tape is in a confirmed uptrend, but valuation is rich (ERP near 0%) and positioning is crowded. Stay at benchmark weight; don't chase.

**US Small/Mid Cap Equities — Overweight.** SMID caps remain a laggard relative to mega-cap tech, carry lower multiples, and benefit disproportionately from a steeper curve, falling oil, and domestic strength. Expected relative catch-up if credit stays benign.

**International Developed (Europe, Japan, UK) — Overweight.** EFA has led vs. the S&P on a 6-month view, EUR/USD is trending higher, and European/Japanese earnings growth has turned positive. Cheaper valuations give a margin of safety. A weaker USD tailwind boosts USD-based returns.

**Emerging Markets — Overweight.** EEM +31% YoY with falling oil a clear tailwind for EM importers (India, Korea, Turkey). China liquidity is improving. Weaker dollar and benign US credit are supportive.

**Gold / Precious Metals — Overweight.** Despite the rally, the drivers (central-bank demand, fiscal concerns, real-rate compression if Fed eases) remain intact. Use as portfolio hedge against an inflation-restart or fiscal-credibility shock. Trim only on clear breakdown.

**Commodities (Oil, Copper) — Neutral.** Oil is now facing supply-normalization headwinds after the ceasefire. Copper remains supported by electrification demand but is vulnerable to a growth scare. Hold benchmark.

**Investment Grade Bonds — Neutral to Slight Overweight.** IG OAS at ~105 bps is tight but coupons of 5–6% are attractive and duration provides ballast against a growth scare. A mild overweight in intermediate IG is reasonable.

**High Yield Bonds — Underweight.** HY spreads at ~285 bps leave minimal compensation for default risk late in the cycle. Better risk/reward in IG or equities.

**Intermediate Treasuries — Overweight.** With the Fed on hold and likely to ease by year-end, intermediate (5–10Y) Treasuries offer a positive carry and capital-gain optionality if growth slows. They are the best recession hedge in the portfolio.

**Bitcoin / Crypto — Neutral.** BTC is down ~11% YoY and has lagged gold badly. Not a bearish call — flows remain structural — but near-term momentum is weak and the narrative has shifted to gold. Maintain a strategic sleeve only.

**Cash / Short Duration — Underweight.** Money-market yields near 4% remain attractive, but with the Fed's next move most likely a cut, reinvestment risk is real. Rotate excess cash into intermediate duration or equity dips.

## Risk Factors to Watch

- **Iran ceasefire breakdown or Strait of Hormuz re-closure.** Would reverse the oil disinflation and hammer international and EM trades.
- **Inflation re-acceleration.** If core CPI follows headline higher (or wages resume rising), the Fed cut path is pushed out. Monitor March PCE (upcoming) and April CPI.
- **Credit spread widening.** HY at cycle lows means any wobble gets amplified. Watch HY OAS; a move above 400 bps would flip the allocation stance to defensive.
- **USD/JPY intervention / Asian FX crisis.** 159+ is in the zone where MOF action becomes probable; a disorderly yen rally has historically triggered global equity selloffs.
- **US fiscal/Treasury supply shock.** 30Y yield re-testing 5% would pressure equity multiples and gold/USD relationships.
