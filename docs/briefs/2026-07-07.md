## Macro Cycle Assessment

The macro backdrop entering July 2026 reflects a late-cycle expansion with growing pockets of resilience and tension. The S&P 500 surged ~1.3% on the week to 7,537, extending its YoY gain to roughly 21% — a remarkable run that increasingly prices in a soft-landing scenario. Yet the bond market is sending a cautionary note: the 10-year Treasury yield edged higher to 4.49%, sitting modestly above year-ago levels despite the Fed having already cut the policy rate by 75bps to 3.75%. This divergence between Fed easing and rising long-end yields suggests the market is beginning to price in either persistent inflation, renewed fiscal concerns, or a term premium re-rating — none of which are benign for duration-sensitive assets. Credit spreads remain historically tight (IG OAS 75bps, HY OAS 274bps), offering little cushion against a risk-off episode.

International developed markets are quietly outperforming this week, with EFA rallying ~1.9% — a continuation of the rotation theme that dominated Q1. EM (EEM +0.2% WoW) is treading water as BTC suffered a sharp ~8.8% week-over-week correction, falling to ~$63,900 from ~$58,559 a week prior (though data shows a rebound from the prior week's trough). Gold is the standout macro signal: spot gold surged to $4,158/oz, up ~3.4% on the week and +24.8% YoY — a powerful signal of persistent demand for hard assets amid fiscal unease and geopolitical hedging. Fund flows into GLD ($+0.38B WoW) confirm the conviction. Cash inflows remain elevated on a 6M basis (+$10.6B into SGOV+BIL), suggesting institutional caution beneath the surface equity optimism.

The macro regime is best characterized as **late-cycle reflation with defensive undercurrents**: equities grinding higher on momentum, but gold surging, long yields rising, small-caps flat, and cash balances high. The Fed is on hold in the near term while fiscal pressures keep term premiums elevated. We are in a phase where selective risk exposure is warranted but broad beta-chasing carries increasing valuation risk, particularly in US large caps. International developed markets and gold remain the most compelling risk-adjusted opportunities, while domestic credit spreads at these tight levels leave high yield exposed to a spread widening episode.

## Allocation Recommendations

**US Large Cap Equities (usLarge) — Slight Underweight (−0.5):** The S&P 500 at 7,537 (+21% YoY) is priced for perfection. Institutional fund flows turned notably negative this week (−$1.67B WoW, −$4.07B MoM into SPY+IVV+VOO), suggesting smart money is trimming exposure into strength. With the 10Y yield rising, real earnings yields are compressing further. We maintain a slight underweight — the trend is up but the risk/reward is deteriorating at these levels.

**US Small/Mid Cap Equities (usSmid) — Neutral (0):** IWM is essentially flat on the week and the MoM flow picture is mixed. Russell 2000 has rallied sharply YoY (+36%) but is stalling. Domestic smid-caps are sensitive to the rate curve and a steepening long end is a headwind. We move to neutral from slight overweight — no clear catalyst to drive outperformance over large-cap or international.

**International Developed Markets (intlDev) — Slight Overweight (+0.5):** EFA rallied nearly 2% this week, extending its YoY gain to ~19%. We maintain a constructive view but temper from the strong overweight of earlier in the year given valuation catch-up.

**Emerging Markets (em) — Neutral (0):** EEM is stable (+0.2% WoW) with improving MoM/6M flow trends. However, a stronger USD environment and rising US long-end yields historically pressure EM. **Gold (gold) — Overweight (+1):** Gold's breakout to $4,158/oz with a +3.4% weekly surge is the most emphatic signal in this week's data. The combination of fiscal deficit concerns, geopolitical hedging, central bank demand, and rising term premiums all favor gold. GLD fund flows are positive this week. ## Risk Factors to Watch

- **Term Premium Re-rating:** 10Y yield rising to 4.49% despite Fed at 3.75% — if long-end yields accelerate above 4.75%–5.00%, this could reprice equities and credit simultaneously
- **Equity Valuation Compression:** S&P 500 at 7,537 with institutional outflows signals distribution risk; a sudden sentiment shift could accelerate a correction in large-caps
- **Bitcoin Momentum Breakdown:** BTC's −41% YoY trajectory and this week's sharp drop may signal broader risk appetite deterioration in speculative assets; watch for contagion into EM and HY
- **Credit Spread Normalization:** IG at 75bps and HY at 274bps are at historically tight levels; any credit event or growth scare could trigger rapid spread widening and significant mark-to-market losses
- **USD Strength Resurgence:** Rising US long-end yields could attract dollar demand, pressuring international developed and EM assets, as well as commodities
- **Geopolitical / Fiscal Shock:** Gold's surge to $4,158 is partly pricing in fiscal and geopolitical tail risk; a materialization of any major shock (trade, Middle East, debt ceiling) could trigger sharp cross-asset volatility
- **Fed Communication Risk:** Any hawkish re-pricing of the Fed path (e.g., sticky CPI) could rattle both bond and equity markets given current tight spread and high valuation conditions
- **Oil Supply Surprise:** WTI near $71.87 is range-bound but an OPEC+ supply cut reversal or demand shock could break crude sharply in either direction, impacting energy-heavy indices and inflation expectations
