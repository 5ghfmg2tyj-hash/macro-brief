## Macro Cycle Assessment

Global risk assets sold off sharply this week, with the S&P 500 declining ~2.6% to 7,383, IWM (Russell 2000) dropping ~3.0% to $281.58, and international markets following suit — EFA fell ~2.4% and EEM tumbled ~5.8%. These moves suggest a coordinated de-risking episode rather than a sector-specific shock, pointing to a broader reassessment of the macro environment. Gold also pulled back meaningfully (−4.9% WoW to $4,339/oz), which is notable given its recent safe-haven status, and Bitcoin cratered from ~$73,755 to ~$60,914 (−17.4%) — both suggesting a liquidity squeeze or margin-call dynamic rather than a simple rotation into defensives.

The macro regime is flashing late-cycle caution. WTI crude rose ~3.4% WoW to $90.31/bbl, keeping energy inflation elevated and complicating the Fed's path toward rate cuts. Credit spreads remain relatively contained — IG OAS at 74 bps and HY OAS at 274 bps, both only modestly wider WoW — suggesting the bond market is not yet pricing in a recession, but the equity and crypto selloff combined with rising energy costs warrants vigilance. Treasury data was unavailable this week, but the absence of a strong flight-to-safety bid in treasuries (flat WoW flows in IEF+TLT) suggests the market is not fully convinced of a risk-off regime shift either.

Fund flows tell a nuanced story: US Large Cap ETFs attracted a solid +$3.5B WoW despite the price decline, suggesting institutional dip-buying, while Cash/Short Duration continues to accumulate (+$10.2B 6M), reflecting persistent risk aversion among retail and institutional investors. Bitcoin and IG bond outflows accelerated. The macro regime is best characterized as a **late-cycle, high-uncertainty environment** with sticky inflation (oil >$90), equity valuations under pressure, and selective safe-haven demand. We lean toward cautious positioning — reducing exposure to rate-sensitive and speculative assets while maintaining selective commodity and quality equity exposure.

## Allocation Recommendations

**US Large Cap Equities (SPY+IVV+VOO) — Slight Underweight (−0.5):** The S&P 500's sharp 2.6% weekly decline despite continued ETF inflows indicates technical vulnerability at elevated valuation levels. Earnings resilience remains a tailwind and the YoY gain of ~23% reflects strong underlying fundamentals, but rising energy costs and a late-cycle macro backdrop justify trimming exposure. We move from Neutral to Slight Underweight, watching whether dip buyers can stabilize the 7,300–7,400 zone.

**US Small/Mid Cap Equities (IWM) — Neutral (0):** Small/mid caps are particularly sensitive to tighter financial conditions and domestic growth deceleration. IWM dropped 3.0% WoW and flows were slightly negative. However, the strong 6-month inflow (+$5.2B) and a robust YoY price gain (+33%) suggest structural interest remains. We reduce from the prior Overweight to Neutral given near-term pressure from higher oil prices and rate uncertainty.

**International Developed Markets (EFA) — Neutral (0):** EFA declined ~2.4% WoW, roughly in line with US large cap. The YoY gain (+13.9%) remains healthy, but 6-month flows are negative (−$0.51B) and the near-term global growth outlook is clouded by energy inflation and China slowdown fears. After maintaining a sustained Overweight, we step down to Neutral to reflect increasing cross-market risk synchronization and fading momentum.

**Emerging Markets (EEM) — Slight Underweight (−0.5):** EEM was the week's worst-performing equity class, dropping 5.8% WoW, with negative 6-month flows (−$0.04B) and month-over-month outflows (−$0.29B). Higher oil prices create a two-tier problem — commodity exporters benefit but importers face stagflationary pressures. Dollar strength is an additional headwind. We reduce from Overweight to Slight Underweight.

**Gold / Precious Metals (GLD) — Overweight (+1):** Gold's 4.9% weekly pullback looks like a deleveraging-driven dip within a powerful structural bull trend — gold is still up ~30.6% YoY and spot price remains at $4,339/oz. The macro backdrop of persistent inflation, geopolitical tension, and central bank diversification away from USD all favor gold on any weakness. We maintain Overweight and view this week's correction as a buying opportunity.

**Commodities — Oil/Copper (WTI) — Overweight (+1):** WTI crude surged to $90.31/bbl (+3.4% WoW, +39.8% YoY), reinforcing the commodity supercycle narrative. Supply constraints and robust global demand (especially in emerging markets) are supporting prices. While this creates macro complications (inflation), it's directionally bullish for commodity exposure. We upgrade to Overweight from Neutral.

**Bitcoin / Crypto (IBIT+FBTC) — Underweight (−1):** Bitcoin's 17.4% weekly crash to ~$60,914 is a significant technical breakdown, and the asset is now down ~41.7% YoY from $104,390. Sustained ETF outflows (−$1.40B over 6 months) confirm institutional retreating. In a risk-off, high-oil-price macro environment, speculative assets face the sharpest headwinds. We downgrade from Neutral to full Underweight.

**Investment Grade Bonds (LQD) — Neutral (0):** IG spreads remain tight at 74 bps (essentially flat WoW) and have compressed significantly vs. a year ago. However, LQD saw −$0.22B WoW and −$0.98B over 6 months in outflows. With 10Y Treasury yields unavailable but presumably elevated given oil/inflation dynamics, duration risk remains a concern. We reduce slightly from Slight Overweight to Neutral.

**High Yield Bonds (HYG+JNK) — Slight Underweight (−0.5):** HY spreads at 274 bps remain historically tight and do not fully compensate for late-cycle default risk should oil-driven inflation damage lower-quality issuers. Outflows over 6 months total −$2.47B. We maintain a cautious lean but reduce our Underweight slightly from full UW (−1) to Slight UW (−0.5), acknowledging that the credit cycle has not yet turned.

**Intermediate Treasuries (IEF+TLT) — Neutral (0):** Treasury yield data was unavailable this week due to a data error, complicating our assessment. However, WoW flows were flat (+$0.00B) despite the equity selloff — suggesting no strong flight-to-safety bid yet. The 6-month outflow of −$1.33B reflects persistent rate uncertainty. With oil above $90 keeping inflation sticky, we reduce from prior Overweight to Neutral.

**Cash / Short Duration (SGOV+BIL) — Slight Overweight (+0.5):** Cash remains a high-conviction defensive allocation. The 6-month inflow of +$10.2B is the strongest of any asset class tracked, and short-duration instruments continue to offer attractive yields in a high-Fed-Funds environment. Given the broad equity selloff, bitcoin crash, and oil-driven inflation overhang, maintaining above-neutral cash is prudent. We hold a Slight Overweight, having dialed back from the prior aggressive Underweight (−1 last week reflects a likely data artifact from the prior week's risk-on tilt).
